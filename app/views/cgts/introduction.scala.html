Capital Gains Tax for non-residents

Capital Gains Tax for non-residents came into effect on 5 April 2015.

If you make or lose money on a UK residential property after that date you need to tell HMRC about it.

You should let them know less than 30 days after you stop owning the property.

You can still be a non-resident if you live in the UK. Read more about residence and tax.

Residential properties

Residential properties can be:

  • houses
  • apartments
  • properties that haven't been built yet

Changing owners

You no longer own a property when you either:

  • sell it
  • give it away
  • swap it
  • get compensation if it's destroyed

Self-assessment

You don't have to pay Capital Gains Tax straight away. You can do it as part of your self assessment return.

However you'll still need to let HMRC know about losses or gains within 30 days.
